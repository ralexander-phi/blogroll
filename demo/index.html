A fresh look at blogrolls.

  • Blogrolls are the social in blogging.
  • A blogroll says who I follow, and suggests writers you might find interesting.
  • Blogrolls are where social media comes from.
  • So now we bring new features from social media back to the world of blogging, via the humble blogroll, of course!
  • To the right, what we call a social blogroll.
  • The blogs appear in the order of their last update, ie when something new was posted.
  • When you click a wedge, links to the five most recent items appear. Click the link to see the post.
  • A blog moves to the top of the list, automatically, in real time when it updates.
  • And of course you can use it to follow news orgs, even social media accounts.
  • And when one of the the blogs in your blogroll updates it moves to the top of the list, and all the expanded stories stay expanded.
  • In other words it goes far beyond where blogrolls were the last time you looked.
  • Behind this prototype is an OPML file, a list of interesting blogs hosted by Automattic. It's a good base of content to get started with. When this is ready as a product, anyone will be able to supply their own list, and display it anywhere, just like a blogroll, which is what it is. :-)
  • Dave Winer, February 2024.
